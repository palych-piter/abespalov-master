My Cairo notes Dec, 2008

It was my second visit to Cairo. First time I went to Cairo with the excursion that I ordered In Hurgada and spent all my time in Cairo with a group of Russian tourists, visiting to National Museum and Great Pyramids. It was quite nice, but I didn't want to have the same trip that time. Moreover, I wanted to visit Alexandria, the city that I hadn't visited during the previous trip. So, I decided to go sight-seeing without a guide that time.







The guide, that accompanied us, said me it was not desirable to spend a lot time in Cairo alone. There aren't many foreigners in Cairo's streets and each foreigner, that Egyptians meet on the streets , can fill abnormal attention. After ten minutes discussion we came to an agreement to exchange cell phone numbers and keep in touch. He explained me how to go to all places I wanted to visit.

As my practice shown, it was true for 90 percent of places in Cairo, but I hadn't been run any risk during my walk . People were taking their looks at me but, in most cases, they were concentrated on they own activities and hadn't enough time to have conversations or spend a time to clarify - who that strange guy was : Only once, a poor boy asked me money and I gave him five Egyptian pounds ( something about 1 US dollar ) . He was contented, it was quite good money for him.

Great Pyramids and National Museum are most popular places where a lot tourists spend time, On the other hand, in other touristic places like The Old Cairo and Citadel, there were touristic groups also from different countries and the special touristic Police taking care of the tourists. Although, in places mentioned, I didn't meet tourists from Russia. All books I sow were in English or German only, It says that Russian tourists usually limit their trips by visiting the Great Pyramids and National Museum. Hmm Е they miss so much.



1. The Old Cairo



The first place I visited was the Old Cairo. This is not a big area located on the Sough of Cairo. I got a taxi and it only took about 15 minutes To go to this interesting place. As you probably know, Islam is the official religion in Egypt. But such state has been from 7-8 centuries when Arabs came to Egypt and brought Islam. Earlier, prior to 7 century, Egypt was the Christian country. So, there are some Christian churches in Egypt that have been stored from these times. There are a lot of these churches in Old Cairo.





2. Church of St. George



I would like to describe couple of Old Cairo remarkable places. The first one is Church of St. George. The old church was built in 530 , At least, there are historical documents dated this year where that place is mentioned. There was a conflagration that destroyed the church in 1904, but the historical building was reconstructed later in 1908. There is St.George icon in the church. People believe that the icon has the same age as the church. They put letters and coins near the icon and believe that brings them a good health and happiness. There is a special room where St.George was tortured. I meet a lot of Greeks walking around the church. Looks like the place is sacred for Greece people.





3. Al-Mullaka Church



Al-Mullaka Church is one of the beautiful places in The Old Cairo. The church was built in nine century and has a lot of old Copt's icons inside. I have spent some time Near the church in a nice church's square and made some people fotos. Please, see the pictures below.



The interesting fact is that recent restoration works had been doing by Russian specialists for some years. There is the legend that Al-Mulaka was built on that very place where the Holy Family hired Jesus from King Irod. Please, take a look at the board at the picture.









4. Mosque of Amr-Ibn-al-As



Near the Old Cairo, there is Mosque of Amr-Ibn-al-As that was built in 641. There were a lot people walking around the church, coming inside in order to pray.

After about 3 hours spending in the Old Cairo, I decided to move to another place and just took a look at the map I bought in advance. The Citadel seemed the place I was interested in. I caught a taxi Е taximan asked who I was , he meant my nationality and was quite surprised to hear that I came from Russia. Looks like Russian people don't visit these places : He said he had thought that I'm looks like Italian Е, got about 5 dollars when had brought me to the place ( The Citadel ) I pointed and was quite happy.





5. Citadel



The place I mentioned was Citadel, one of the beautiful places I have seen, located on the top of a mountain, over the city. Standing near the Citadel you can see the Great Pyramids ( please take a look at the cycled part of the picture provided ). Whole the Great Cairo at you feet, it is really fantastic view. There were a lot children walking in the square near the Mosque. I was interested why so much children are in the same place and asked waiter I met in the cafe I came to drink cold water.

He mentioned that it was happened on Fridays when children didn't visit schools but spent a lot time near Mosques walking, playing, eating snacks at all. I have not seen so many children in one place previously. I spent about 3 hours in Citadel. Then took the taxi to go to the place my guide mentioned to meet my group and go to the hotel, The taxman spoke English a bit and brought me to the necessary place.
